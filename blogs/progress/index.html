∫ A thought on progress

Kernel:

  • A progress is made when we achieve hard things with reliability.

Over the past month, I have been immersed in a challenging task that required extensive trial and error to complete. This experience reminded me of how we, as humans, strive to make progress within our society.

When pursuing goals and achievements, it's crucial to consider which endeavors are worthwhile and which are not.

It's often said that only difficult tasks are worth pursuing. But how do we define difficulty? One possible criterion is the rarity of a task's occurrence. The rarity may stem from either a limited number of ways to achieve the goal, or the probability of success being inherently low for all available paths.

In the first case, progress is made when we identify the path or even better alternatives. In the second case, simply finding a path or the most optimal one isn't enough; we must also marshal significant resources to generate enough successful outcomes to claim mastery.

This brings up two important questions:

  • What should an individual do? Should they choose an easily achievable goal and focus on finding the most reliable way to reach it, or venture further and seek a path, reliable or otherwise, to attain more challenging objectives?
  • Who will garner more acclaim? Those who uncover simple solutions and share them with everyone, or those who can accomplish difficult tasks?
